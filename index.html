4. Systems for Management Groups

Different levels of management require different types of information systems to make decisions.

Senior Management (Strategic)

Focus: Long-term planning, unstructured problems.

System: ESS (Executive Support Systems)

Middle Management (Tactical)

Focus: Monitoring, control, semi-structured decisions.

System: MIS & DSS
Example: Airline Pricing DSS (Analyzing competitor prices & history).

Operational Management (Day-to-day)

Focus: Routine, specific transactions.

System: TPS (Transaction Processing System)
Example: Retail Inventory (Recording shirt sales).

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

Integrates all key business processes (Finance, HR, Manufacturing) into a single system.

Real-Life Example
Tesla: Uses ERP to manage everything from ordering battery materials to tracking the final car warranty.

The Power of Integration: When fully integrated, a sale in CRM triggers the ERP to check inventory, which triggers SCM to order more parts automatically.
